In 1924, miners prospecting near Mount St. Helens claimed to be attacked one night by several large, hairy "ape men" that pelted rocks from above. They shot at the creatures and said the attack lasted all night before escaping at dawn. Some skeptical investigators think it could have actually been another group of miners playing a prank on them.

In 1924, Albert Ostman said while prospecting in a remote forest he was picked up in his sleeping bag by a large male Bigfoot and carried back to their camp. He claimed a family held him captive for days and fed him vegetation before he freed himself. But many wonder if Ostman fabricated elements or the entire tale.
